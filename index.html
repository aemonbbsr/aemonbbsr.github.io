Rock Timeline

Journey through the defining moments and evolution of rock's golden era

1967

Psychedelic Rock Emerges

The Beatles release "Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band," revolutionizing studio production and album-oriented rock, while Jimi Hendrix debuts "Are You Experienced," redefining what was possible on the electric guitar.

1969

Woodstock Defines a Generation

The Woodstock Music & Art Fair becomes the defining cultural moment for a generation, featuring iconic performances from Jimi Hendrix, The Who, and Jefferson Airplane, establishing the template for the rock festival experience.

1970

Hard Rock Takes Center Stage

Black Sabbath releases their self-titled debut album, widely considered the first true heavy metal album, while Led Zeppelin's "Led Zeppelin III" showcases the band's expanding musical palette.

1973

The Birth of Arena Rock

Pink Floyd releases "The Dark Side of the Moon," pioneering prog-rock's expansive sound, while bands like Led Zeppelin and The Who perfect the stadium rock experience with elaborate stage shows and sound systems.

1977

Punk Challenges the Establishment

The Sex Pistols release "Never Mind the Bollocks," while The Clash debuts their self-titled album, offering raw, politically charged alternatives to the perceived excesses of progressive rock and disco.

1981

MTV Revolutionizes Music Promotion

MTV launches with "Video Killed the Radio Star," forever changing how rock music was marketed and consumed, creating a new emphasis on visual presentation and leading to the rise of glam metal.

1984

Hair Metal Dominates

Bands like MÃ¶tley CrÃ¼e, Poison, and Whitesnake define the era with their combination of power ballads, guitar virtuosity, and glam aesthetics, bringing rock to unprecedented commercial heights.

1987

Appetite for Destruction Changes Everything

Guns N' Roses releases their debut album, bringing a raw, dangerous edge back to hard rock and setting the stage for the grittier sounds that would define the early 90s.

1991

Grunge Emerges from Seattle

Nirvana's "Nevermind" and Pearl Jam's "Ten" bring the Seattle sound to the mainstream, challenging the dominance of hair metal and reconnecting rock with its raw, emotional core.

1994

The End of an Era

Kurt Cobain's death marks a symbolic turning point for rock, as alternative bands like Green Day, The Offspring, and Weezer bring punk-influenced rock back to the charts with a more polished sound.

1999

Rock Splinters Into Subgenres

As the decade closes, rock fragments into nu-metal, post-grunge, and pop-punk, while older rock acts like Aerosmith and Rolling Stones continue strong touring careers, bridging generations of rock fans.

Speaking the Language of Rock

Ever been confused by terms like "whammy bar," "power trio," or "stadium anthem"? Our Rock Lexicon helps newcomers understand the terminology that defines rock's golden era. Consider this your essential guide to speaking like a true rock aficionado.

Whammy Bar

Also called a tremolo arm or vibrato bar. A device attached to the bridge of an electric guitar that allows the player to quickly vary the tension and pitch of the strings, creating a vibrato or diving effect.

Instruments
"Eddie Van Halen's dive bombs with the whammy bar revolutionized guitar playing in the late 70s."

Double-Neck Guitar

A specialized electric guitar with two necks, typically a 6-string and a 12-string, allowing guitarists to switch between different sounds without changing instruments.

Instruments
"Jimmy Page's double-neck Gibson EDS-1275 became iconic during live performances of 'Stairway to Heaven'."

Marshall Stack

A full amplification setup consisting of a Marshall amplifier head atop one or more speaker cabinets, known for their distinctive powerful sound and visual impact on stage.

Instruments
"The wall of Marshall stacks behind AC/DC created both an impressive visual and earth-shaking volume."

Power Chord

A simplified two-note chord consisting of the root note and its fifth, often played with distortion. The foundation of most rock rhythm guitar playing.

Techniques
"Black Sabbath's 'Iron Man' opens with one of rock's most recognizable power chord progressions."

Shredding

Playing guitar with high-speed, technically demanding passages, often featuring sweep picking, tapping, and legato techniques.

Techniques
"Eddie Van Halen's shredding technique on 'Eruption' inspired a generation of guitarists."

Headbanging

A physical response to music where listeners vigorously move their head in rhythm with the beat, particularly associated with heavier rock and metal genres.

Techniques
"The synchronized headbanging of Metallica fans created a sea of movement during their arena shows."

Glam Metal

Also known as hair metal, a subgenre that combined hard rock with pop hooks and an androgynous, theatrical visual style including elaborate hairstyles, makeup, and flashy costumes.

Genres
"Poison and MÃ¶tley CrÃ¼e exemplified the glam metal aesthetic that dominated MTV in the mid-1980s."

Progressive Rock

A complex subgenre characterized by unusual time signatures, extended compositions, technical instrumental proficiency, and often conceptual or fantasy themes.

Genres
"Yes and King Crimson pioneered progressive rock's intricate arrangements and virtuosic performances."

Arena Rock

A style characterized by anthemic choruses, polished production, and songs designed to work in large stadium settings, often featuring audience participation elements.

Genres
"Journey's 'Don't Stop Believin'' remains the quintessential arena rock anthem decades after its release."

Groupie

A fan who follows a band on tour and often seeks personal relationships with band members. Though sometimes used pejoratively, many were significant cultural figures in their own right.

Culture
"Famous groupies like Pamela Des Barres became cultural icons who documented rock's backstage history."

Backstage Pass

A credential allowing access to restricted areas at concerts, particularly the area behind the stage where artists prepare and relax before and after performances.

Culture
"Backstage passes became coveted collectibles, often featuring unique artwork for each tour."

Record Store Day

An annual event inaugurated in 2008 to celebrate independent record stores, featuring special vinyl releases and performances that has helped revive interest in physical music formats.

Culture
"Many classic rock albums are reissued with bonus material for Record Store Day, connecting new fans to the vinyl era."
